Key Findings

  • No-RAG wins (F1=0.9423) — granite-4.1-30b has sufficient medical knowledge to evaluate standard NBME concepts without retrieval. McNemar's test: p=6.55×10⁻⁷ vs Naive RAG.
  • Naive RAG fails on Case 202 — bulk retrieval causes feature-ID hallucination (890 phantom FPs). LLM generates malformed concept IDs when given cross-case synonym context.
  • Agentic RAG underperforms due to early tool-call termination by granite-4.1-30b, not an architectural flaw. A stronger instruction-following model would close the gap.
  • Negation concepts are the Achilles heel — "No X" findings score F1=0.829 vs 0.954 for positive findings, across all strategies.
